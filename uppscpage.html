What's UPPSC AE Exam ?


The UPPSC stands for Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission. This is a government established that aims at providing state-level government jobs to the youth of Uttar Pradesh. UPPSC AE is a recruitment examination conducted for the recruitment of eligible and able candidates for the post of Assistant Engineer.Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission conducts entrance exams every year for the post of Assistant Engineer in the State Services. This year, UPPSC is conducting Entrance Exam for Assistant Engineer (AE) to fill a total of 712 vacancies under general recruitment from various engineering fields for various departments of the state. Majority of the seats will be filled by the students from Civil, Mechanical and Electrical Discipline of Engineering.

Conducting body : Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission Assistant Engineer is a state-level exam conducted in the state of Andhra Pradesh. Public Service Commission conducts the UPPSC AE exam for entry into the civil services of Uttar Pradesh.

UPPSC-AE Age Criteria :
  • Minimum Age Requirement: 21 Years of age
  • Maximum Age Requirement: 40 Years of age
Relaxation in Age Criteria:
  • A relaxation of (born after 2nd July 1974) five years for candidates belonging to
    Scheduled Castes of U.P.
    Scheduled Tribes of U.P.
    Other Backward Classes of U.P.
    Skilled players of U.P. of Classified Games
    State Govt. Employees of U.P. including Teachers/Staff of the Basic ShikshaParishad of U.P.
    Teachers/Staff of the Government Aided MadhyamikVidyalayas of U.P.
  • A relaxation of fifteen years for Physically Handicapped persons of U.P
  • A relaxation of five years for Emergency Commissioned Officers/ Short Service Commissioned Officers/Ex-Army Personnel’s of U.P. who have rendered five years service in Army (no reservation for Group- 'B' posts)



UPPSC AE 2020 Required Educational Qualifications To Be Eligible :

Assistant Engineer (Civil/Mechanical)(Irrigation department):

  • Essential: Candidate must have the civil or mechanical engineering degree from a recognized university or institution or should be a qualified Associate Member of the Institute of Engineer in the following branch – civil engineering or mechanical engineering.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served a minimum 2 years of period in the Territorial Army or must have completed one-year training or must have obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Engineer (Agriculture/Civil/ Mechanical) (Minor Irrigation department):

  • Essential: Candidate should have a bachelor degree in the following fields – agriculture/ civil/ mechanical from a recognized university or institution or passed Associate Member Examination in civil or mechanical engineering.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served a minimum 2 years of period in the Territorial Army or must have obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Engineer (Civil) (Rural Engineering Department):

  • Essential: Candidates should have a bachelor degree in civil engineering from a recognized university or institution or passed the Associate Member Examination in civil engineering.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served a minimum 2 years of period in the Territorial Army or must have obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Engineer (Civil) (Public Works Department (P.W.D.) and Mandi Parishad):

  • Essential: Candidates must have passed a bachelor degree from a university established by law in India or recognized by government.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served a minimum 2 years of period in the Territorial Army or must have completed one-year training or must have obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Engineer (Electrical/ Mechanical) (Public Works Department (P.W.D) and Mandi Parishad):

  • Essential: Candidates must have passed a bachelor degree in electrial or mechanical engineering from a recognized university or university established by law in India or passed Associate examination from Imperial College of Science and Technology South, Kesingston in electrial and mechanical engineering or passed section A and B in electrial or mechanicl engineering of Associate Membership of Institution of Engineers/ Institutions of Engineers.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served a minimum 2 years of period in the Territorial Army or must have obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C or equivalent to that.

Assistant Engineer (Housing and Urban Planning Department):

  • Essential: Candidates must possess a degree in civil engineering from a recognized university.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served a minimum 2 years of period in the Territorial Army or must have obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C or equivalent to that.

Assistant Engineer (Electrical/ Mechanical):

  • Essential: Candidates must possess a degree in electrical or mechanical engineering from a recognized university.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served a minimum 2 years of period in the Territorial Army or must have obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Engineer (Civil) (Nagar Vikas Vibhag):

  • Essential: Candidate must have passed sections ‘A’ and ‘B’ of the associateship examination or passed a degree in civil engineering from a recognized university or diploma of Institutions of Engineers.
  • Preferential: Candidate who has served minimum 2 years in the Territorial Army or obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Engineer (Electrical/ Mechanical) (Nagar Vikas Vibhag):

  • Essential: Candidate must possessed a degree in electrical or mechanical engineering from a recognized university accredited by Institutions of Engineers or passed sections ‘A’ and ‘B’ of the associateship examination in electrical or mechanical engineering.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served minimum 2 years in the Territorial Army or obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Engineer (Water)/’B’ Category Jalkal Engineer (Nagar Vikas Vibhag):

  • Essential: Candidate should have a degree in electrical or mechanical engineering from a recognized university accredited by Institutions of Engineersor passed sections ‘A’ and ‘B’ of the associateship examination in electrical or mechanical engineering.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served minimum 2 years in the Territorial Army or obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Engineer (Civil) (Medical Department):

  • Essential: Candidates must have a bachelor degree in civil engineering from a recognized university or established by law or its equivalent to that.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served minimum 2 years in the Territorial Army or obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C. At the time of direct requirement, andidates who are a trainee on Assistant Engineer (Civil) post will get more preference.

Engineer (Panchayati Raj Department):

  • Essential: Candidate must possess a bachelor degree in civil engineering from a recognized university or established by law.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served minimum 2 years in the Territorial Army or obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

U.P Agriculture Service Group “B” (Engineering Branch)(AgricultureDepartment):

  • Essential: Candidate must possess a bachelor degree in agriculture engineering from a recognized university or established by law.
  • Preferential: Candidate must possess a post graduate degree in agriculture engineering from a recognized university or established by law or who have served minimum 2 years in the Territorial Army or obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Bhoomi Sanrakshan Adhikari/Technical Officer (Irrigation and Water Resources department):

  • Essential: Candidate must possess a bachelor degree in agriculture or civil engineering from a recognized university or established by law.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served minimum 2 years in the Territorial Army or obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C and must have a diploma in soil conservation and water management.

Assistant Director, Electrical Security (Energy department):

  • Essential: Candidate must have a bachelor degree in electrical engineering from a university established by law or must be an associate member of the Institution of Engineers in electrical engineering.
  • Preferential: Candidates who have served minimum 2 years in the Territorial Army or obtained a “B” certificate of N.C.C.

Assistant Director (Factories) (Labour department): Candidates must have passed 12th examination from the Board of High School and Intermediate Examination or an examination recognized by the government or equivalent to that. A candidate must possess a degree in Engineering or Technology in one of the following branches – Civil/ Mechanical/ Electrical/ Chemical/ Industrial Engineering from a recognized university or a university established by law.

Assistant Director (Boilers) (Labour department):Candidate must have a bachelor’s degree in mechanical engineering from a recognized university or university established by law or equivalent to that. Candidates should have a minimum of 2 years of practical training in construction design, operation or maintenance of boilers.

Exam Pattern



The examination shall be conducted according to the following plan:—

  1. Stage-I: Engineering Services (Preliminary/Stage-I) Examination (Objective Type Papers) for the selection of candidates for the Stage-II: Engineering Services (Main/Stage-II) Examination;
  2. Stage-II: Engineering Services (Main/Stage-II) Examination (Conventional Type Papers) and
  3. Stage-III: Personality Test

Stage-I(Preliminary/Stage-I) Examination :-


  • The Engineering Services (Preliminary/Stage-I) Examination will consist of two objective type (multiple choices) questions papers and carrying a maximum of 500 marks (Paper 1 – 200 Marks & Paper II – 300 Marks)
  • IES Prelims exam will be conducted in offline mode i.e. it is a pen and paper-based test.
  • Candidates are provided OMR sheets for both the papers, wherein the correct answer needs to be colored. 
  • For each wrong answer, one-third mark is deducted.
  • If a candidate gives more than one answer, it will be treated as a wrong answer even if one of the given answers happen to be correct and there will be same penalty as above for that question.

Papers Number Of Question Duration Of Exam Maxmium Marks
Paper 1 (General Studies & Engineering Aptitude) 100 2 hours 200
Paper 2 (Engineering Discipline) 150 3 hours 300
Total 250 Questions 5 hours 600 marks

Stage-II(Main/Stage-II) Examination:-


  • The Engineering Services (Main/stage-II) Examination will consist two conventional type papers in Engineering Discipline specific with duration of three hours and maximum marks of 600 (300 Marks in each paper).
  • IES Stage 2 will have two papers. Both the papers will have questions from the opted Engineering discipline (Civil Engineering/ Mechanical Engineering/ Electrical/ Electronics & Telecommunication Engineering).
  • There will be 8 questions as an option, out of which you will have to attempt 5 questions.
  • There will be no negative marking in this exam.
  • Candidates have to answer in English language only.
  • Up to 5% marks may be deducted in case of illegible handwriting.
  • Credit will be given for exact, to the point answer using an economy of words.
IES Main Papers Number of Questions Duration of Exam Maximum Marks
Paper 1 (Engineering Discipline) 5 out of 8 Optional Questions 3 hours 300
Paper 2 (Engineering Discipline) 5 out of 8 Optional Questions 3 hours 300
Total 10 Questions 6 hours 600 marks


Stage-II(Main/Stage-II) Examination:-


  • This stage is also known as Personality Test.
  • It is the final stage in IES recruitment exam.
  • Only candidates qualifying the written exams are called for the interview round.
  • The candidates are scored out of 200 marks.
  • In IES Interview round, the following skills of the candidates areassessed:
    • Suitability of candidate for a career in the public service sector
    • Mental caliber or Mental alertness
    • Leadership Qualities
    • Initiative & Intellectual Curiosity,
    • Mental & Physical energy and
    • Other social traits and interestsin current affairs.
