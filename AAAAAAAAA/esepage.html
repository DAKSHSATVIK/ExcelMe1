What's ESE(IES) Exam ?


The Engineering Services Examination (ESE) remains the most sought-after career for the engineering graduates in India. Engineering Services Examination commonly known as ESE is conducted annually by UPSC to recruit engineers in four domains namely Civil, Mechanical, Electrical, Electronics & Telecommunications for the techno managerial posts.The ESE exam is considered as one of the toughest examinations in the country. Nearly, two lakh candidates appear for the examination every year. Engineering Services Exam or ESE exam recruits the Group A and Group B officers at the technical positions for Government of India. The ESE Exam is a three-stage recruitment process - ESE Preliminary, ESE Main and Personality Test. On the basis of merit of candidates in ESE exam, they are recommended to the Union Government for the available posts. Nature of work for an engineering officer selected through ESE exam depends on the engineering branch and the cadre s/he is recruited for.However, the selected candidates can move to any cadre, organisation, agency, department, ministry and PSU of the Government of India. All these final selected candidates are designated as class-1 officers.The first position offered is that of Asst. Executive engineer and the hierarchy ends at the position of Chairman/ Managing Director. Read more at: https://www.careerindia.com/upsc/ies-exam-e26.html


Candidates after the final selection are posted in various Ministries and Departments of GOI such as Railways, Telecom, Border Roads, CPWD, CWC, CPES, NHAI, Naval Armaments, IDSE, MES, Ordnance Factories etc.

Conducting body : Indian Engineering Services (IES) exam, also known as Engineering Services Examination (ESE), is a national level recruitment exam organised by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC). The exam is conducted to recruit candidates for engineering positions for the various department working under the Government of India.In the past, this exam had been conducted with the name of Indian Engineering Services(IES) exam.

Candidates should not be born earlier than January 2, 1990 and not later than January 1, 1999.The Upper age limit of 30 years is relaxable up to 35 years in case of government servants serving.

Minimum marks required to appear : To be eligible for the exam you need to have a B.E/ B. Tech. Student should be an Indian. In graduation you need to have a percentage minimum of 50% in the graduation degree.

Exam Pattern



The examination shall be conducted according to the following plan:—

  1. Stage-I: Engineering Services (Preliminary/Stage-I) Examination (Objective Type Papers) for the selection of candidates for the Stage-II: Engineering Services (Main/Stage-II) Examination;
  2. Stage-II: Engineering Services (Main/Stage-II) Examination (Conventional Type Papers) and
  3. Stage-III: Personality Test

Stage-I(Preliminary/Stage-I) Examination :-


  • The Engineering Services (Preliminary/Stage-I) Examination will consist of two objective type (multiple choices) questions papers and carrying a maximum of 500 marks (Paper 1 – 200 Marks & Paper II – 300 Marks)
  • IES Prelims exam will be conducted in offline mode i.e. it is a pen and paper-based test.
  • Candidates are provided OMR sheets for both the papers, wherein the correct answer needs to be colored. 
  • For each wrong answer, one-third mark is deducted.
  • If a candidate gives more than one answer, it will be treated as a wrong answer even if one of the given answers happen to be correct and there will be same penalty as above for that question.

Stage-II(Main/Stage-II) Examination:-


  • The Engineering Services (Main/stage-II) Examination will consist two conventional type papers in Engineering Discipline specific with duration of three hours and maximum marks of 600 (300 Marks in each paper).
  • IES Stage 2 will have two papers. Both the papers will have questions from the opted Engineering discipline (Civil Engineering/ Mechanical Engineering/ Electrical/ Electronics & Telecommunication Engineering).
  • There will be 8 questions as an option, out of which you will have to attempt 5 questions.
  • There will be no negative marking in this exam.
  • Candidates have to answer in English language only.
  • Up to 5% marks may be deducted in case of illegible handwriting.
  • Credit will be given for exact, to the point answer using an economy of words.
IES Main Papers Number of Questions Duration of Exam Maximum Marks

Stage-II(Main/Stage-II) Examination:-


  • This stage is also known as Personality Test.
  • It is the final stage in IES recruitment exam.
  • Only candidates qualifying the written exams are called for the interview round.
  • The candidates are scored out of 200 marks.
  • In IES Interview round, the following skills of the candidates areassessed:
    • Suitability of candidate for a career in the public service sector
    • Mental caliber or Mental alertness
    • Leadership Qualities
    • Initiative & Intellectual Curiosity,
    • Mental & Physical energy and
    • Other social traits and interestsin current affairs.
